HouseSeatc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/house-membership/HouseSeat

A seat in a house. A place reserved for a member in a house. This does not equate to a constituency. A seat might be associated with a constituency (in the House of Commons) but might be allocated to a bishop or inherited by an hereditary peer.
has sub-classes
LawLordHouseSeatc, SpiritualHouseSeatc
is in domain of
hasHouseSeatTypeop, hasSeatIncumbencyop, representsConstituencyGroupop
is in range of
hasHouseSeatop

HouseSeatTypec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/house-membership/HouseSeatType

A house seat type. This includes first past the post elected in the Commons. In the Lords this includes hereditary peers, life peers, excepted hereditary peers and Lords spiritual (made up of named diocesan and long serving English diocesan bishops).

hasHouseSeatTypeop
