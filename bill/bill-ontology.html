Carry overc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/bill/CarryOver

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/bill/
Public Bills which have not been passed by the end of the session in which they were introduced will not progress into the next session unless a carry over motion has been agreed. A bill being carried over results in the creation of a new bill in the new session, the text of the new bill being the text of the original bill as amended in the previous session. Private Members' Bills cannot be carried over.
is in domain of
Carried over fromop, Carried over toop

A hybrid bill is a public bill having general application across the whole population, whilst also having been examined by the Examiners of Petitions for Private Bills and found to differently affect the interests of particular groups, people or places. Parliament Act procedure invocationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/bill/ParliamentActProcedureInvocation

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/bill/
Where a public bill is passed by the House of Commons and rejected by the House of Lords in the same session, the Commons may introduce an identical bill in the following session under the procedure set out in the Parliament Act 1911. Minor changes may exist between the bill as amended in the first session and the bill as introduced in the second session if such changes are certified by the Speaker of the Commons. If the House of Lords reject the second bill, that bill may be put forward for Royal Assent if the Speaker of the Commons certifies that the bill meets the conditions set out in section 2 of the 1911 Act.
is in domain of
Parliament Act invoked fromop, Parliament Act invoked toop
