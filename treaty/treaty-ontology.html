A series of which a treaty is an item by means of a treaty series item. The United Kingdom Foreign and Commonwealth Office lists treaties in one of four series: the bilateral series, the multilateral series, the European Union series or the Treaty Series. One of the four treaty series is itself called the Treaty Series.

Treaty series itemc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/treaty/TreatySeriesItem

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/treaty/
A treaty is listed in a treaty series as a treaty series item. At any time, a treaty series item is always in a treaty series. A treaty may be placed in a new treaty series by means of a new treaty series item. A treaty is listed in one of the bilateral, multilateral or European Union treaty series if it has not come into force in the United Kingdom. A treaty subject to the Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010 is listed in one of these series when first laid before Parliament. If Parliament permits ratification of the treaty, the treaty becomes available to the government to ratify and then may come into force. When a treaty comes into force, it is listed in the Treaty Series.
is in domain of
In seriesop, Series item citationdp
is in range of
As series itemop
