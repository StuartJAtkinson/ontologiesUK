Made statutory instrument workc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/MadeStatutoryInstrumentWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/
Made statutory instruments may progress from draft, or be laid without draft before Parliament as made. Parliament may consider particular instruments as made with imposing a duty to lay. For example: commencement orders. Made statutory instruments may be objected to within Parliament. A made instrument has been signed into secondary legislation by a government minister. Made statutory instruments are published by The National Archives and classified by them as a FRBR work.

Proposed draft remedial order workc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/ProposedDraftRemedialOrderWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/
A proposal for a draft remedial order. Under paragraph 3(1) of schedule 2 of the Human Rights Act 1998, the Minister has a duty to lay before Parliament 'a document which contains a draft of the proposed order'. This document must sit before Parliament for a period of 60 days, beginning with the day on which it was laid.

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/ProposedNegativeStatutoryInstrumentWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/
A regulation - made under a power or powers set out in clauses 7, 8 or 9 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 - which government ministers propose be laid under the negative procedure. Sifting committees of either House may recommend that any subsequent statutory instrument or instruments be laid under the affirmative procedure.

Published draft under the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 workc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/PublishedDraftUnderEUWAWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegated-legislation/
A draft published under the duties laid out in paragraph 14 of schedule 8 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018. A Minister has a duty to publish such a draft 28 joint sitting days before the laying of any instrument which intends to amend or revoke subordinate legislation under section 2(2) of the European Communities Act 1972, including subordinate legislation implementing EU directives.
