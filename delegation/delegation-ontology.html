Draft statutory instruments are laid before Parliament subject to either a negative, affirmative or super affirmative procedure. Made statutory instrument workc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegation/MadeStatutoryInstrumentWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegation/
Made statutory instruments as signed into legislation. Made statutory instruments may result from a draft laid before Parliament or may be laid as made or may not be laid if the enabling legislation does not set out a duty to lay. Made statutory instruments laid before Parliament may be subject to a negative, affirmative or super affirmative procedure, or none. A made negative, having not been objected to within the scrutiny period, remains law. A made affirmative or made super affirmative, having been approved by Parliament within the approval period, remains law.
has super-classes

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegation/ProposedDraftRemedialOrderWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegation/
A proposal for a draft remedial order. Under paragraph 3(1) of schedule 2 of the Human Rights Act 1998, the Minister has a duty to lay before Parliament 'a document which contains a draft of the proposed order'. This document must sit before Parliament for a period of 60 days, beginning with the day on which it was laid.

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegation/ProposedNegativeStatutoryInstrumentWork

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/delegation/
A regulation - made under a power or powers set out in clauses 7, 8 or 9 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 or under schedule 5 of the Future Relationship Act 2020 - which government ministers propose be laid under the negative procedure. Sifting committees of either House may recommend that any subsequent statutory instrument or instruments be laid under the affirmative procedure.
