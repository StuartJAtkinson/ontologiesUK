Parliamentary paper ontology

A model describing parliamentary papers, their types and their presence in bundles and in series.

A parliamentary paper as a single intellectual work, as defined in the Functional Requirements for Bibliographic Records specification.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

A parliamentary paper made available to a House.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

A group of related parliamentary papers, called a bundle, for example: a bundle containing a statutory instrument paper and a related explanatory memorandum.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

The type of a paper, for example: bill, statutory instrument, petition or statement of annual accounts.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

A parliamentary paper, as it exists as part of a paper series. A parliamentary paper forming part of a series has a number within that series.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

A series of parliamentary papers, for example: the Treaty Series.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

A period during which Parliament is able to conduct business on days of its choosing. A session is within a Parliament period. The first session of a Parliament begins on the same day as the Parliament period and ends on a day named in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the Parliament period begin and end on dates named in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The final session of a Parliament ends either with prorogation followed by dissolution or with dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.
