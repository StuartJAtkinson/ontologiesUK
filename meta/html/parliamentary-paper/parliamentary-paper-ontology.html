Parliamentary paper ontology

A model describing parliamentary papers, their types and their presence in bundles and series.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Paper bundle

A bundle of related parliamentary papers, for example: a bundle containing a statutory instrument paper and its related explanatory memorandum.

Class Paper series

A series of parliamentary papers, for example: the Treaty Series.

Class Paper series item

A parliamentary paper as it exists as part of a paper series. A parliamentary paper forming part of a series may result in the paper being 'numbered' as part of that series.

Class Paper type

The type of a paper, for example: a bill, a statutory instrument, a petition, a written statement or a set of annual accounts.

Class Parliamentary paper

The expression of a parliamentary paper work as made available to a House.

Class Parliamentary paper work

A parliamentary paper made available to Parliament as realised in an expression made available to a House.

Class Session

A period during which Parliament is able to conduct business on days of its choosing. A session is within a Parliament period. The first session of a Parliament begins on the same day as the Parliament period and ends on a day named in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the Parliament period begin and end on dates named in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The final session of a Parliament ends either with prorogation followed by dissolution or with dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.

Object properties

Object property Belongs to

Relates a paper series to the session that series belongs to. A paper series may belong to one session, or none.

PaperSeries — Belongs to — Session

Object property Expression of

Relates a parliamentary paper to the work the paper is an expression of. A parliamentary paper is an expression of one work. A parliamentary paper work may be expressed by one or more parliamentary papers.

ParliamentaryPaper — Expression of — ParliamentaryPaperWork

Object property Forms part of

Relates a parliamentary paper to the paper bundle it forms part of. A parliamentary paper may belong to one or more paper bundles, or none.

ParliamentaryPaper — Forms part of — PaperBundle

Object property Has type

Relates a parliamentary paper to its type. A parliamentary paper may have one or more types.

ParliamentaryPaper — Has type — PaperType

Object property In series

Relates the existence of a parliamentary paper in a series to the series it forms part of. A paper series item forms part of one paper series.

PaperSeriesItem — In series — PaperSeries

Object property Paper series follows

Relates a paper series to its predecessor series. A paper series may follow one series, or none.

PaperSeries — Paper series follows — PaperSeries

Object property Paper series parent

Relates a paper series to its parent paper series. A paper series may have one parent paper series, or none. A paper series may have one or more child paper series, or none. For example: the Treaty Series of in force treaties forms part of the overarching series grouping all treaties.

PaperSeries — Paper series parent — PaperSeries

Object property Paper type parent

Relates a paper type to its parent paper type. A paper type may have one or more parent paper types, or none. A paper type may have one or more child paper types, or none.

PaperType — Paper type parent — PaperType

Object property Positions parliamentary paper

Relates the existence of a parliamentary paper in a series to that parliamentary paper. A paper series item refers to one parliamentary paper.

PaperSeriesItem — Positions parliamentary paper — ParliamentaryPaper

Relates a paper series to the prefix forming part of the 'number' of a parliamentary paper being part of that series.
