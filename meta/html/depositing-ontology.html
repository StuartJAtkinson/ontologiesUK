Depositing ontology

A model describing the commitment to make and the making of a deposit in the Library of either House.

Classes · Object properties · Data properties · Subclasses · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class Availability

The type of availability of a deposited paper, being: available to the public, available to the public in a redacted form, or available only to Members.

Class Commitment

A commitment to make a deposit in a Library of a House.

Class Committing thing

A thing by which a commitment to deposit is made, for example: a contribution to a parliamentary debate, a letter to a Library or a written statement.

Class Credited contribution

An oral contribution made by a Member during a parliamentary proceeding.

Class Deposited artefact

An artefact having been deposited in a Library of a House.

Class Depositing

The act of placing information in the Library of either House, resulting from a commitment to make a deposit of that information, by a Member permitted to do so.

Superclasses · MakingAvailable

Class Incumbency

The holding of a position by a person, including the time period for which this was the case. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Class Made available thing

A thing made available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Made statutory instrument work

Made statutory instruments may progress from draft, or be laid without draft before Parliament as made. Parliament may consider particular instruments as made with imposing a duty to lay. For example: commencement orders. Made statutory instruments may be objected to within Parliament. A made instrument has been signed into secondary legislation by a government minister. Made statutory instruments are published by The National Archives and classified by them as a FRBR work.

Class Making available

The act of making a thing available to a House or to a House by means of its Library.

Class Paper expression

A paper made available to a House, being an expression of a paper work.

Class Paper series item

A paper expression or artefact, as it exists as part of a paper series. A paper expression or artefact forming part of a series has a number within that series.

Class Parliamentary period

The time period of a single Parliament between the date of its first assembly after a general election and its dissolution. A Parliament period contains contiguous session and prorogation periods. During a parliament period Parliament is either in session or prorogued. The start date of a Parliament may be changed by subsequent proclamations. A Parliament ends at the next dissolution. While the Fixed-term Parliaments Act 2011 was in force, the date of dissolution was determined by that Act or was named in a proclamation following a vote in Parliament for an early general election. The next Parliament began on the date named in the proclamation issued following a dissolution. Before the Fixed-term Parliaments Act 2011 came into force and after that Act was repealed by the Dissolution and Calling of Parliament Act 2022, the date of dissolution is fixed by proclamation. The dissolution proclamation also names the date for Parliament to reassemble. Parliament periods are the same across both Houses. Parliaments are numbered ordinally from the first Parliament of the United Kingdom, sitting in 1801.

Object property Artefact as series item

Relates an artefact to the existence of that artefact in a paper series. A paper series item refers to one artefact. An artefact may exist as an item in one or more series, or none.

Object property Has availability

Relates a deposited paper to the availability of that deposited paper. A deposited paper has one availability.

DepositedPaper — Has availability — Availability

Object property Leads to

Relates a credited contribution in which the intention to make a commitment is recorded to the thing in which that commitment is made. Credited contributions may include the phrase, 'I will write'. A 'will write' is a contribution intended to lead to a letter of commitment to the Library, that letter being the committing thing. A letter of commitment may result from one credited contribution, or none.

CreditedContribution — Leads to — CommittingThing

Object property Made during

Relates a commitment to the parliamentary period during which the commitment was made. A commitment is made during one parliamentary period.

Object property Makes commitment

Relates a committing thing to the commitment made. A committing thing makes one commitment. A commitment is made in one committing thing.

Object property Results from commitment

Relates a deposit to the commitment to make that deposit. A deposit results from one commitment, or none. A commitment is made to provide one or more deposits.

Depositing — Results from commitment — Commitment

Object property Results from duty set out in

Relates a deposit to the made statutory instrument setting out the duty under which the deposit is made. Transport and Work Act orders are deposited under a duty set out in Rule 13 of The Transport and Works (Applications and Objections Procedure) (England and Wales) Rules 2006.

Object property Results in deposited artefact

Relates an act of depositing to the artefact or artefacts having been deposited. An act of depositing results in one or more deposited artefacts, or none. A deposited artefact is the result of one deposit.
