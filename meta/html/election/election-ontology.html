Election ontology

Classes | Object properties | Data properties | Subclasses | Namespaces | Depiction
A model describing incumbencies resulting from general elections and by-elections for seats in the House of Commons, by-elections for excepted hereditary peers in the House of Lords and elections for positions including that of the Speaker of the House of Commons. The model describes the result of an election process, rather than the process by which an election has been conducted: election returns by the “first past the post” system and those which follow other methods are included. Where applicable, the model is aligned with the Cabinet Office Election Result Data Standard.

Classes

# Candidacy class

A person standing as a candidate in an election, for example: Phillip Hammond standing as the Conservative Party candidate in Runnymede and Weybridge, at the 2015 General Election.

# Candidacy result class

The result of a person’s candidacy in an election.

# Certification class

A certification of a candidate by a political party, registered by the Electoral Commission, to stand in an election on behalf of that party. A candidate may be certified by more than one party, for example: by both The Labour Party and The Co-operative Party.

# Constituency area class

A geographic bounding of a constituency group.

# Constituency area type class

The type of constituency area, for example: a borough or a county.

# Constituency group class

A group of people represented by a person who has won an election, including people other than those eligible to vote at the time of election.

Superclass: Population.

# Election class

An election to elect a candidate or candidates to a seat or position.

# Electoral incumbency class

An incumbency in a position or parliamentary seat decided by the result of an election.

# Electorate class

A group of people who are eligible to vote in an election called at a particular time, for example: the group of people entitled to vote in a constituency election, or members of the House of Commons voting for their Speaker.

Superclass: Population.

# General election class

A set of elections to seats in the House of Commons held in all UK constituencies concurrently.

# Parliament period class

The time period of a single Parliament between the date of its first assembly after a general election and its dissolution. A Parliament period contains contiguous session and prorogation periods: during a parliament period Parliament is either in session or prorogued. Until 2015, the dissolution proclamation named the date for Parliament to reassemble. After 2015, a Parliament begins on the date named in the proclamation issued after a dissolution. The start date of a Parliament may be changed by subsequent proclamations. A Parliament ends at the next dissolution. Until 2015 the date of dissolution was indeterminate and fixed by proclamation. After 2015 the date of dissolution is determined by the Fixed Term Parliament Act 2011, or is named in a proclamation after Parliament has voted for an early general election. Parliament periods are the same across both Houses. Parliaments are numbered ordinally from the first Parliament of the United Kingdom, sitting in 1801.

# Person class

A person.

# Political party class

A group registered with the Electoral Commission as a political party.

# Population class

A set of people.

# Winning candidacy result class

Identifies a winning candidacy in an election.

Superclass: CandidacyResult.

Data properties

Ballots spoiled data property

The number of spoiled ballots cast by an electorate in an election, for example: ballots on which votes are given for more candidates than the voter is entitled to vote for, ballots on which anything is written or marked by which the voter can be identified, or ballots which are unmarked or void for uncertainty.

Election — Ballots spoiled — integer

Election — Declaration time — dateTime

Total votes cast data property

The total number of votes cast across all candidacies in an election, including spoiled ballots. Turnout is defined by the House of Commons Library as the total number of votes cast and not spoiled, divided by the size of the electorate. This definition conforms to the generally accepted academic usage. Turnout is generally defined by Returning Officers as the total number of votes cast divided by the size of the electorate.

Election — Total votes cast — integer

Vote count data property

CandidacyResult — Vote count — integer

Vote share data property

The share of a vote won in an election by a candidate as a fraction of the accepted votes.

CandidacyResult — Vote share — decimal
