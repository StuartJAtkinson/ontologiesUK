Laying ontology

A model describing the process of laying papers before a House and any subsequent withdrawal and replacement.

Subclasses

laying: LayingOrWithdrawal

laying: Withdrawal
is a subclass of
laying: LayingOrWithdrawal

Classes

House class

A parliamentary House, for example: the House of Lords.

Laid paper class

A paper having been laid before a House. Papers and supporting documents may be laid as a bundle.

Laying class

The act of making available a paper and any supporting documents into a House. Papers may be laid before both Houses on the same day.

LayingOrWithdrawal (superclass) → Laying (class)

Laying authority class

The authority under which a paper is laid before a House. Legislation may impose a duty on a body to lay papers before a House, these are laid 'by Act'. Command papers are laid in a House by the government and are laid 'by Command'. A paper is laid in the House of Commons or House of Lords either 'by Act' or 'by Command'.

Laying body class

A body competent to lay papers before a House or Houses.

Laying duty class

The duty of a body to lay papers before a House as imposed by an Act of Parliament.

Laying or withdrawal class

The act of laying a paper before a House or withdrawing a paper from a House.

Person class

A person.

Session class

The period during which Parliament is convened. The first session of a parliament begins on the same day as the parliament period: this date is given in the dissolution proclamation at the end of the previous parliament. The session ends on the day set in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the parliament period begin and end on dates set in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The last session of a parliament ends with prorogation followed by dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.

Withdrawal class

The act of withdrawing a paper previously laid before a House.

LayingOrWithdrawal (superclass) → Withdrawal (class)

Withdrawn paper class

A paper withdrawn after having been laid. Papers or documents forming part of a bundle may be individually withdrawn.

Object properties

Has House object property

Relates an act of laying or withdrawing a paper to the House in which the paper is laid or withdrawn.

Domain: LayingOrWithdrawal
Range: House

Has laying authority object property

Relates an act of laying a paper to a laying authority.

Domain: Laying
Range: LayingAuthority

Has laying body object property

Relates an act of laying or withdrawal a paper to the laying body laying or withdrawing it.

Domain: LayingOrWithdrawal
Range: LayingBody

Has laying duty object property

Relates an act of laying a paper to a duty to lay papers under an Act.

Domain: Laying
Range: LayingDuty

Has laying person object property

Relates an act of laying a paper to the person laying it.

Domain: Laying
Range: Person

Has session object property

Relates an act of laying or withdrawing a paper to the session which the paper is laid into or withdrawn from. Papers laid during a prorogation period are considered laid into the session which has been prorogued.

Domain: LayingOrWithdrawal
Range: Session

Lays object property

Relates an act of laying a paper to the paper which has been laid.

Domain: Laying
Range: LaidPaper

Replaces object property

Relates a laid paper to the withdrawn paper being replaced.

Domain: LaidPaper
Range: WithdrawnPaper

Withdraws object property

Relates an act of withdrawing a paper to the paper which has been withdrawn.

Domain: Withdrawal
Range: WithdrawnPaper

Data properties

Laid on data property

Relates an act of laying a paper to the date on which the laying occurs. This date falls within a parliamentary session or prorogation period, and not during a dissolution period.

Laying (domain) → Laid on → date (range)

Withdrawn on data property

Relates an act of withdrawal of a paper to the date on which the withdrawal occurs. This date falls within a parliamentary session or prorogation period, and not during a dissolution period.

Withdrawal (domain) → Withdrawn on → date (range)
