Laying ontology

A model describing the process of laying papers before a House and any subsequent withdrawal and replacement.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

The period during which Parliament is convened. The first session of a parliament begins on the same day as the parliament period: this date is given in the dissolution proclamation at the end of the previous parliament. The session ends on the day set in a subsequent prorogation proclamation. Subsequent sessions within the parliament period begin and end on dates set in subsequent prorogation proclamations. The last session of a parliament ends with prorogation followed by dissolution. Session periods are the same for both Houses.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

The act of making available a paper and any supporting documents into a House. Papers may be laid before both Houses on the same day.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

The holding of a position by a person, including the time period for which this was the case. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Class LABEL PLACEHOLDER

The authority under which a paper is laid before a House. Legislation may impose a duty on a body to lay papers before a House, these are laid 'by Act'. Command papers are laid in a House by the government and are laid 'by Command'. A paper is laid in the House of Commons or House of Lords either 'by Act' or 'by Command'.

A paper having been laid before a House. Papers and supporting documents may be laid as a bundle.
