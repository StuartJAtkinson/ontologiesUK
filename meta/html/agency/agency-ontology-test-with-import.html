Classes

Concept class

Group class

A group of people. Groups may contain other groups or be empty.

Agent (superclass) → Group (class)

Group inclusion class

The inclusion of a person or group in another group over a period of time, for example: a membership of a committee or a whipping to a party.

Incumbency class

The holding of a position by a person, including the time period for which this was the case. For example, Philip Hammond holding the position of Foreign Secretary between two dates.

Place class

A place named in a geography.

Position class

A named post within a group. A position may be held by one or more people over time or at the same time, or have no holder at a particular time: for example, the post of Minister of Food, having existed in the past and having had eight successive incumbents; or a Member of the House of Commons and a Member of the House of Lords both serving as Co-Chairs of the Joint Committee on the Palace of Westminster.

Has expertise in object property

Relates an agent to a concept to express an area of expertise. An agent may have one or more areas of expertise, or none.

Agent (domain) → Has expertise in (object property) → core#Concept (range)

Has group inclusion object property

Relates an agent to a group inclusion. An agent may have one or more group inclusions, or none. A group inclusion is to a single agent.

Agent (domain) → Has group inclusion (object property) → GroupInclusion (range)

Has incumbency object property

Relates a person to an incumbency. A person may have one or more incumbencies, or none. An incumbency is to a single person.

Person (domain) → Has incumbency (object property) → Incumbency (range)

Has interest in object property

Relates an agent to a concept to express an area of interest. An agent may have one or more areas of interest, or none.

Agent (domain) → Has interest in (object property) → core#Concept (range)

Has position object property

Relates an incumbency to a position. An incumbency is to a single position. A position may have one or more incumbencies, or none.

Incumbency (domain) → Has position (object property) → Position (range)

In group object property

Relates a group inclusion to a group. A group inclusion is in a single group. A group may have one or more group inclusions, or none.
