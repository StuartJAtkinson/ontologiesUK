Treaty ontology

A model describing the form of treaties considered by Parliament, treaty membership of an FCO series and the relationship of a treaty to legislation required to be passed in order for the UK to comply with treaty obligations.

The National Archives classify made instruments as legislation - and draft instruments as draft legislation. Parliament considers both made and draft instruments as part of the legislative process, distinct from a FRBR work as used by TNA. Parliament includes draft instruments in its description of statutory instruments, which are modelled here together.

# Treaty as laid class <<<<<<< HEAD =======
#TreatyAsLaid >>>>>>> ff70d49b7b552a728eeeee7d4798e9bdada211db

Treaties requiring ratification - including those treaties requiring ratification to which the United Kingdom is acceding - are subject to the Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010. The 2010 Act sets out that such treaties must be laid before Parliament prior to ratification and are then subject to a procedure described in part within the Act. All treaties are laid before Parliament following ratification, though this is not a requirement set out in the 2010 Act and does not trigger a parliamentary procedure.

# Treaty series class <<<<<<< HEAD =======
#TreatySeries >>>>>>> ff70d49b7b552a728eeeee7d4798e9bdada211db

A series of which a treaty is an item by means of a treaty series item. Treaty series are defined by the Foreign and Commonwealth Office. The Country Series lists bilateral agreements which are not yet in force. The European Union Series lists multilateral agreements between member states of the European Union, or between third parties and member states of the European Union, which are not yet in force for the UK. The Miscellaneous Series lists multilateral agreements where the UK is a signatory and which are not yet in force. A treaty subject to the Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010 is listed in one of the Country, European Union or Miscellaneous series when first laid before Parliament. Once a treaty has entered into force it is published in the FCO Treaty Series. The Treaty Series lists agreements that have completed all necessary procedures and are now in force for the UK.

# Treaty series item class <<<<<<< HEAD =======
#TreatySeriesItem >>>>>>> ff70d49b7b552a728eeeee7d4798e9bdada211db

A treaty is published in a treaty series as a treaty series item. At any time, a treaty series item is always in a treaty series. A treaty may be placed in a new treaty series by means of a new treaty series item. Treaty series are defined by the Foreign and Commonwealth Office. The Country Series lists bilateral agreements which are not yet in force. The European Union Series lists multilateral agreements between member states of the European Union, or between third parties and member states of the European Union, which are not yet in force for the UK. The Miscellaneous Series lists multilateral agreements where the UK is a signatory and which are not yet in force. A treaty subject to the Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010 is listed in one of the Country, European Union or Miscellaneous series when first laid before Parliament. If Parliament does not object to ratification of the treaty, the treaty becomes available to the government to ratify and then may come into force. Once a treaty has entered into force it is published in the FCO Treaty Series. The Treaty Series lists agreements that have completed all necessary procedures and are now in force for the UK.

Object properties

As series item object property

Relates a treaty as laid before Parliament to a treaty series item. A treaty series item places a treaty in the treaty series it is listed in at the time of laying. Each treaty series item refers to one treaty as laid. A treaty as laid appears as one treaty series item

TreatyAsLaid — As series item — TreatySeriesItem

Has lead department object property

Relates a treaty to its lead government department. Treaties are laid into Parliament by the Foreign and Commonwealth Office and may have a different assigned lead department.

Treaty — Has lead department — GovernmentOrganisation

In series object property

Relates a treaty series item to the treaty series in which it appears. A treaty series item is listed in a single treaty series.

TreatySeriesItem — In series — TreatySeries

Laid as object property

The 'Laid as' property relates a treaty to the form in which it is laid. The 2010 Act sets out that such treaties must be laid before Parliament prior to ratification and are then subject to a procedure described in part within the Act. Data properties

Command Paper number data property

A treaty is laid as a Command Paper, having a citation formed of a Command Paper prefix and Command Paper number. Within a series, Command Paper numbers start at 1 and do not exceed 9999.

TreatyAsLaid — Command Paper number — Literal

Command Paper prefix data property

A treaty is laid as a Command Paper, having a citation formed of a Command Paper prefix and Command Paper number. A series of Command Papers has a unique prefix.

TreatyAsLaid — Command Paper prefix — Literal

Series item citation data property

An item in a treaty series has a citation, in addition to a Command Paper number: for example, China No. 1 (2017).

TreatySeriesItem — Series item citation — Literal

UK coming into force date data property

The date on which a treaty is recorded as coming into force. Parts of a treaty may come into force on different dates and under differing conditions. Where no single coming into force date is given, coming into force details are given in a coming into force note.

Treaty — UK coming into force date — date

UK coming into force note data property

A coming into force note sets out details applying to the UK of the commencement of provisions or effects forming part or parts of a treaty, or the treaty as a whole where it does not come into force on a single date.

Treaty — UK coming into force note — langString

UK ratification date data property

The date on which the treaty is ratified by the UK.
