House membership enabling ontology

A model attaching House seats and seat incumbencies to the things they are enabled by.

Classes · Object properties · Namespaces · Depiction

Classes

Class House seat

A seat as a position in a House - rather than a physical seat or constituency - which may be allocated to a Member, for example: a seat in the House of Commons associated with a constituency - or a seat in the House of Lords allocated to the Archbishop of Canterbury, inherited by a hereditary peer or granted to a life peer.

Class House seat enabling thing

Class Seat incumbency

The occupation of a seat in a House by a person, for a period of time. A person may have more than one incumbency over time. Only one person can be incumbent in a house seat at one time. A person having a seat incumbency which has a start date in the past and an end date in the future - or which has no end date - is a current Member. A person having a seat incumbency with an end date in the past - and no current incumbency - is an ex-Member.
