AllocatedAnsweringBodyc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/AllocatedAnsweringBody

The answering body allocated to a question, either at tabling or when raised in the House, or in the event of the question being transferred between answering bodies. Note that the allocated answering body can change over time when one answering body transfers responsibility to another answering body. So allocated answering bodies are time bound. This would use startAt and endAt properties from the Core ontology.

allocatedToop
is in range of
allocationop, transferredFromop, transferredToop

AnswerExpectedAsc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/AnswerExpectedAs

A time-bound means of defining the type of answer (oral or written) expected for a question. Sometimes a question in pursuit of an oral answer will be scheduled to be asked in the chamber, but the member intending to ask the question will be unable to attend the chamber at that time. The question will then be removed from the schedule (referred to as unstarred) and instead become a question in expectation of a written answer. Such a question would have two Answer Expected As relationships: one for the initial period when the answer type expected is oral and one for the second period when the answer type expected is written.
is in domain of
isOralAnswerExpecteddp
is in range of
answerExpectedAsop

BusinessQuestionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/BusinessQuestion

Business Questions allow members to ask the Leader of the House about upcoming business in the House. Unlike Parliamentary Questions, the text of a Business Question will be read in the chamber. ParliamentaryQuestionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/ParliamentaryQuestion

Parliamentary Questions (often referred to as PQs) allow members to ask for information on the work, policy and activities of answering bodies. Usually this is to a minister in a government department but sometimes questions can be asked of non-departmental bodies. e.g. Church Commissioners. Parliamentary questions can be asked in expectation of either a written or an oral answer (one spoken by a member in the chamber). Unlike urgent and business questions, Parliamentary Questions must be tabled with the Table Office rather than raised with the Speaker.
has super-classes
Questionc

UrgentQuestionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/UrgentQuestion

Urgent Questions allow members of the House of Commons to ask a minister in a government department about matters which demand immediate action or attention. In the House of Lords questions with the same purpose are referred to as Private Notice Questions. Unlike Parliamentary Questions, the text of an Urgent Question or Private Notice Question will be read in the chamber. The answer will also always be oral. Urgent Questions and Private Notice Questions are not tabled with the Table Office but raised with the Speaker.
has super-classes
Questionc

answerToop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/answerTo

Allows an answer to be associated with one or more questions. This replicates more precisely the current functionality around grouped questions. In other words, a group of questions in receipt of the same answer. There is some debate as to whether the same text given in answer to questions from multiple Members can be considered one, or the same, answer. This also allows a single question to have many answers. Sometimes multiple answers from a single Answering Body (e.g. holding answer, current answer, correction). Sometimes different answers from different Answering Bodies to the same question e.g. answers to a round robin question. Note that this is a major change to current data models, where a single question can have one and only one answer, and a single answer can be to one and only one question.
has domain
Answerc
has range
Questionc

isQuestionSubstantivedp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/isQuestionSubstantive

Determines if a question is substantive. If true the question text will be unique and meaningful and constitute the actual question that the member wished to ask. If false the question text will be some boilerplate asking about a Government Department or the Prime Minsiter's appointments. This will be answered in the chamber, after which the asking member will ask a supplemental question which is the question they intended to ask in the first place. This is done so the answering member does not have foresight of the actual question before the question and answer session. It only applies to Parliamentary Questions expecting an oral answer. Non-substantive questions are usually referred to as topical.
has domain
Questionc
has range
boolean

namedDateForAnswerdp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/question-and-answer/namedDateForAnswer

Relates a Parliamentary Question to a named day. The member tabling the question has requested that it be answered by the named day. Note that named day questions can only be tabled in the House of Commons. Most written questions are 'ordinary'. By convention a member can expect such questions to be answered within 7 days of tabling. Named day questions are used to elicit a faster response.
has domain
Questionc
has range
date
